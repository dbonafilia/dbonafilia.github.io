Most recently, I've been working on AI powered mapping and remote sensing to contribute to open source maps at Facebook. # Projects ## World's Most Accurate Population Density Maps(HRSL) ![]( {{ "assets/popdensimage.gif"" | relative_url }}) The High Resolution Settlement Layer(HRSL) is the world's most accurate population density map. Working as an engineer on the World.AI team at Facebook, I found a novel combination of semi-supervised and weakly supervised learning that vastly improved the accuracy of the world's most accurate population density maps. This work was [published in CVPR 2019's Computer Vision for Global Challenges Workshop] (https://research.fb.com/publications/building-high-resolution-maps-for-humanitarian-aid-and-development-with-weakly-and-semi-supervised-learning/https://research.fb.com/publications/building-high-resolution-maps-for-humanitarian-aid-and-development-with-weakly-and-semi-supervised-learning/) and explained in detail in a pair of blog posts published on Facebook's [AI](https://ai.facebook.com/blog/mapping-the-world-to-help-aid-workers-with-weakly-semi-supervised-learning/) and [Tech](https://tech.fb.com/ai-powered-maps-help-vaccination-campaigns/) blogs. This worked involved automatically scaling a training data set from around 1 million iamges to 100's of millions of images and improving the accuracy of the final model by more than 12%. {% endcapture %} {{md | markdownify }}
